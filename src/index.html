ZONE 2
Aerobic Base / Fat Burn
115 – 134
65–75% HRR  ·  bpm
When to use
Long steady runs, easy rides, base-building phases. Most of your weekly volume should be here.
Feel
Comfortable — you can speak in short sentences. Slightly aware of your breathing.
Training tips
The foundation of endurance fitness. Aim for 60–70% of weekly training time here. Builds mitochondrial density.
Fuel source
~65% fat · 35% carbs. Prime fat-burning zone.
ZONE 3
Aerobic Tempo
134 – 150
75–84% HRR  ·  bpm
When to use
Tempo runs, moderate group rides, sustained efforts of 20–60 min
Feel
Moderate — breathing harder, conversation is difficult. Comfortably uncomfortable.
Training tips
Use sparingly (10–15% of volume). Called the "grey zone" — tiring but less adaptive than Z2 or Z4. Best as race-pace work.
Fuel source
~50% fat · 50% carbs. Balanced burn, glycogen demand rising.
ZONE 4
Lactate Threshold
150 – 163
84–93% HRR  ·  bpm
When to use
Interval training, hill repeats, 5K–10K race pace efforts, threshold blocks (3–8 min)
Feel
Hard — can only say a few words. Burning sensation in muscles. Sustainable for ~10–15 min.
Training tips
1–2 sessions/week max. Raises your lactate threshold = you go faster before fatigue hits. Structure as intervals (e.g. 5×4 min).
Fuel source
~25% fat · 75% carbs. High glycogen demand — fuel before.
ZONE 5
VO₂ Max / Neuromuscular
163 – 189
93–100% HRR  ·  bpm
When to use
Sprint intervals, HIIT, short maximal efforts (30 sec–2 min), sport-specific speed work
Feel
All-out — cannot speak. Maximum effort. Difficult to sustain beyond 2 minutes.
Training tips
Max 1×/week for intermediates. Use very short intervals (30s–90s) with full rest. Boosts VO₂ max and power.
Fuel source
~5% fat · 95% carbs. Almost pure glycolysis. Carb-up beforehand.

Intermediate Training Tips

🏃 80/20 Rule

Keep ~80% of your training in Zones 1–2 and only ~20% in Zones 3–5. Most intermediates do the opposite — and plateau.

📏 Measure Accurately

A chest strap HR monitor is far more accurate than optical wrist sensors, especially during intervals. Consider one for Zone 4–5 work.

🔄 Progression

Build Z2 base for 6–8 weeks, then layer in one Z4 session/week. Add Z5 only after threshold improves.

💤 Recovery Signals

If your resting HR is 5–7+ bpm above normal in the morning, take a Zone 1 day or full rest — you're not fully recovered.

🍽️ Fueling by Zone

Z1–2: no extra carbs needed. Z3: light snack before. Z4–5: carb-rich meal 2–3 hrs prior and recovery shake after.

📈 Track & Adjust

As fitness improves, your zones shift — retest every 8–12 weeks. A lower resting HR means your zones will recalculate slightly.
